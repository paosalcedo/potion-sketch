poetry flight

Creative Challenge

Our Prototype Studio class at NYU was given a selection of assets, which included a headless 3D model of a person, several short stories from Sir Arthur Conan Doyle. The only constraint was that we had to use assets from this selection and nothing else. Aside from that, the sky was the limit.
I decided to indulge my growing interest in using code to do fun stuff with words.

Creative Response

“Poetry Flight” is a game about deconstructing and reconstructing text. Paste text such as actual poetry, obnoxious tweets, and annoying news articles, then experience it in a whole new flight.

Check out this project's GitHub repo.

voyeur at the edge of the universe

Creative Challenge

“Voyeur at the Edge of the Universe” was my final project for Worlds On A Wire– an NYU ITP class taught by Todd Bryant. I wanted to prototype a way to experience stories in VR without moving the camera independent of the player– a common cause of VR-induced dizziness or nausea.

Creative Response

“Voyeur” contains three different scenes or rooms, each with a different story; the player can control when or if each story progresses– kind of the way a DJ would mix and match different musical tracks in a live performance. Rather then requiring nausea-inducing camera movements or jarring teleportaion/scene changes to reveal the world to the viewer, the entire fictional world is presented akin to a dollhouse, or Polly Pockets from the '90s. Made with MagicaVoxel, Mixamo, and Unreal Engine 4.

CoMix

Creative Challenge

The challenge was to use AR as a narrative tool.

Creative Response

CoMix is an AR comics experiment that lets you change the story when you rearrange the panels. I wanted to create a speculative AR project that would let comic book readers enjoy the benefits of AR without sacrificing the joy and tactility of holding the printed page–or panels, in this case. Many thanks to Nico Zapanta for the “Action Star” art. Made in Unity with Vuforia.
